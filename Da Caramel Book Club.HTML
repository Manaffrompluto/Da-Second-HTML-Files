-Introduction:About How Mummies Are Made

Many people including Egypt and many other countries in the ancient times mummified people, especially Pharoahs and wealthy people. First, they would take out their organs, including the brain through the nose but excluding the heart as it was believed to be needed in the afterlife world and then storing the organs in special canopic jars. After that, they would cover the body with a special salt called natron for 40 days to dry the bodies. Then, the bodies were carefully wrapped in linen. Then, they put an amulet on the mummy for afterlife protection. Last of all, resin was used to seal the linen and the body was put in a coffin to be entombed. And, the process took roughly 70 days.
